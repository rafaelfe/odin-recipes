Tapioca With Cheese and Coconut

Tapioca with Cheese and Coconut is a delectable Brazilian street food that seamlessly melds the rich traditions of indigenous culinary heritage with contemporary flavors. Rooted in the cassava plant, indigenous to Brazil, tapioca pearls or starch create the base for this delightful dish. This street food sensation showcases the chewy, translucent texture of tapioca, coupled with the creamy allure of melted cheese and the tropical sweetness of coconut.

INGREDIENTS

Yield: 4 to 6 servings

  • 1 cup tapioca flour (also known as tapioca pearls or starch)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • Butter or coconut oil for cooking

PREPARATION

Tapioca Dough:

  • Place the tapioca flour in a bowl.
  • Slowly add water while stirring continuously until you have a moist but not sticky dough consistency.

Cook the Tapioca Pancake (Crepe):

  • Heat a non-stick skillet or pan over medium heat.
  • Pour half of the tapioca dough onto the pan, spreading it out evenly to form a thin pancake.
  • Cook for 2-3 minutes or until the edges start to lift and the bottom is golden brown.
  • Flip the tapioca pancake and cook the other side for an additional 1-2 minutes.

Add Cheese and Coconut:

  • Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over one half of the tapioca pancake.
  • Sprinkle half of the sweetened shredded coconut over the cheese.

Fold and Finish:

  • Carefully fold the tapioca pancake in half, covering the cheese and coconut filling.
  • Press gently with a spatula and let it cook for an additional minute or until the cheese is melted.

Repeat for the Second Serving:

  • Repeat the process to make the second tapioca pancake with cheese and coconut.

Serve:

  • Once both tapioca pancakes are ready, transfer them to a cutting board and cut each into halves or quarters.
  • Serve warm and enjoy the gooey, cheesy, and coconut-infused goodness.
